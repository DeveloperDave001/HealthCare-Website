BENUE STATE PRIMARY HEALTH CARE BOARD

Introduction
The State Primary Health Care Board (SPHCB) is one of the parastatals under the State Ministry of Health, responsible for the delivery of efficient and effective primary health care services. It was established by an Act of the Benue State House of Assembly on 16 July 2012. Gazette No.12 Vol. 38 of 28th March 2013. The cardinal goal is to provide a single administrative framework and managerial processes (that is bringing Primary Health Care Under One Roof (PHCUOR)) , for revitalizing and repositioning of PHC services in the State. The essence is to revolve programmes that are designed to prevent diseases, promote health, general medical care, maternal and child health care, family planning, rehabilitation, nursing care and home visits, etc.

Vision
To be a national best practice Agency effectively promoting and supporting problem-oriented health systems research as a tool for the provision of essential healthcare services.

Mission
To build a high-performing organization with tested management principles and promote collaboration with other stakeholders in creating the enabling environment for reduction of disease burden, maternal and neo-natal deaths and uptake of immunization and vaccination against killer diseases

Core Values
The core values intended to drive the vision, mission and the objectives are:
• Transparency and Accountability
• Availability of and access to quality, equitable and affordable primary health care services.
• Protection from financial hardship in seeking healthcare.
• Standards and efficiency in primary health service delivery.
• Gender equity and responsiveness, cultural sensitivity, professionalism and maintenance of ethical standards in primary health service delivery.
• Equity and Networking.

Objectives
The objectives of the SPHCB are:
• To coordinate the implementation of primary health care programmes and activities by bringing “Primary Health Care Under One Roof” in the state.
• To ensure equitable distribution and adequate maintenance of Primary Health Care facilities and effective access to services in the state.
• To work with other relevant bodies at National, State and LGA levels to facilitate the implementation of the National Health Policy and National Health Act in the state.

Management Structure
The BNSPHCB, is constituted of sixteen (16) Board members, which have oversight functions on its operations. The daily management of the organization rests with the Executive Secretary acting as the Chief Executive Officer.

In accordance with the mandate and statutory functions of the SPHCB, the operating arms, which are the various Departments at the State and Local Government Health Authorities (LGHAs) are charged with the responsibilities of facilitating and coordinating interventions and programs of BNSPHCB for effective and efficient achievement of the goals of the Primary Health Care Delivery System.

Structure/Directorates
The SPHCB, has six Directorates and an Internal Audit Unit all headed by Directors. Namely:
• Directorate of Planning, Research, Statistics and Monitoring & Evaluation
• Directorate of Disease Control and Immunization
• Directorate of Essential Drugs and Logistics
• Directorate of Community and Family Health Services
• Directorate of Administration and Human Resources
• Directorate of Finance and Accounts.
• Internal Auditor

The departments serve as the fulcrum through which the BHCPB performs its stewardship role and other statutory responsibilities necessary for achieving the goals and objectives of PHCUOR.
